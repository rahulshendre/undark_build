# Debts Recovery Tribunal (DRT)

## When used
For recovery of debts by **banks and financial institutions** above a high
pecuniary threshold — a specialized tribunal track faster than ordinary courts
for large claims.

## Who files
A bank or notified financial institution (via authorized representative). Note:
not every NBFC qualifies — eligibility depends on the Act/notifications.

## Eligibility (why it does NOT fit small-ticket)
- The claim must be **at or above the minimum threshold**. Small tickets are far
  below it. TODO: verify current minimum (commonly cited as **₹20 lakh**).
- The applicant must be an eligible bank/financial institution.

## Required documents
- Loan agreement and statement of account
- Computation of dues
- Security documents (if any)
- Authorization

## Expected timeline
Designed to be faster than ordinary civil courts for large debts; actual times
vary. TODO: verify.

## Limitations
- **Hard threshold** — sub-threshold claims (including all sub-₹1 lakh cases) cannot be filed here.
- Limited to eligible applicant institutions.

## Typical use cases
- Large secured/unsecured debts of banks/FIs — **outside** Undark's segment.

## Decision tree
```
Is the claim ≥ DRT threshold AND applicant an eligible bank/FI?
  ├─ yes → DRT
  └─ no  → not available → Lok Adalat / NI Act / Order 37 / civil suit
```

## Common mistakes
- Assuming DRT is an option for a small-ticket case (it is not — below threshold).
- Wrong assumption that any NBFC can file (eligibility is specific).

## Operational takeaway for Undark
Like SARFAESI, the useful output for the core segment is the **negative**: DRT is
out because the amount is below threshold. Confirm and move to the realistic
routes.
