# Lok Adalat

## When used
To settle a money-recovery dispute by compromise, either **pre-litigation** or
for a **pending** case. The realistic default for small-ticket unsecured
recovery where the borrower is reachable and a negotiated figure is achievable.

## Who files / initiates
Either party can seek a reference. In practice the lender/practitioner initiates
— via the relevant Legal Services Authority (for pre-litigation) or by getting a
pending court case referred.

## Eligibility
Any compoundable / compromisable money matter the parties are willing to settle.
An **ordinary** Lok Adalat only records a settlement reached by consent — it does
not decide on merits if the parties do not agree. (A **Permanent** Lok Adalat
under §22-B can adjudicate for notified public-utility services — usually not the
small-ticket consumer-loan path.)

## Required documents
- Loan agreement / sanction letter
- Statement of account and a clean computation of dues
- KYC of borrower (and guarantor, if any)
- Copy of demand / pre-litigation notice + proof of dispatch
- Authorization/board resolution for the person appearing
- Any cheque/PDC or security documents, if relevant

## Expected timeline
Fast — settlements are typically reached within a single sitting. National Lok
Adalats are held periodically alongside regular sittings. TODO: verify current

## Limitations
- Requires the borrower's **consent** to settle; no settlement → no award (ordinary Lok Adalat).
- The award records the agreed terms; it does not impose a contested decision.
- Borrower must be locatable and willing to engage.

## Typical use cases
- Unsecured personal / MFI / consumer loans below SARFAESI and DRT thresholds.
- Cases where the practitioner wants a fast, low-cost, final closure.
- Written-off accounts where any recovery is upside and settlement room is wide.

## Decision tree
```
Sub-₹1 lakh, unsecured?
  └─ Borrower reachable & possibly willing? ── yes ─→ Lok Adalat (settlement)
       └─ Borrower unreachable / refuses? ── consider:
            ├─ Cheque bounced?            → NI Act §138 (legal/ni-act.md)
            ├─ Clear written debt / PN?   → Order 37 summary suit
            └─ Otherwise                  → ordinary civil suit
```

## Common mistakes
- Walking in without a **reconciled** outstanding figure (amount must tie to documents).
- No prior **written notice / dispatch proof** — weakens credibility and the trail.
- Setting an unrealistic settlement ask that kills the consent.
- Forgetting authorization for the person appearing.

A Lok Adalat award is **final and binding**, has the status of a **civil court
decree**, and is **not appealable**; court fee paid (if any) is typically
