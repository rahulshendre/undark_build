# NI Act Section 138 — cheque dishonour

## When used
When the borrower gave a cheque (including a security/post-dated cheque) that is
**dishonoured** for insufficient funds or "exceeds arrangement". One of the
strongest levers in small-ticket recovery because it carries criminal liability.

## Who files
The **payee / holder in due course** (the lender, via its authorized
representative/practitioner) files a criminal complaint before the Magistrate.

## Eligibility
- A cheque was issued to discharge a legally enforceable debt/liability.
- It was presented within validity and **dishonoured**.
- The strict statutory notice-and-wait steps are followed (below).

## Required documents
- The dishonoured cheque
- Bank's cheque-return memo (dishonour intimation)
- Copy of the statutory demand notice + proof of dispatch/service
- Loan agreement / proof of the underlying debt
- Authorization to file on the lender's behalf

## Expected timeline (STRICT — verify exact counts)
1. On dishonour, send a **demand notice** to the drawer within **30 days** of receiving the bank's dishonour intimation.
2. The drawer gets **15 days** from receipt of the notice to pay.
3. If unpaid after 15 days, file the complaint within **30 days** of that period expiring.

## Limitations
- Only available if a cheque actually exists and bounced — no cheque, no §138.
- Timelines are unforgiving; a late notice or late complaint usually defeats it.
- It is a criminal lever to compel payment, not a fast civil decree; recovery of the money may still need separate/parallel steps.

## Typical use cases
- Loans backed by security/PDC cheques (common in NBFC/MFI lending).
- Where the borrower can pay but is stalling — the criminal exposure drives settlement.

## Decision tree
```
Cheque issued for the debt?
  ├─ no  → §138 not available → Lok Adalat / Order 37 / civil suit
  └─ yes → was it dishonoured?
       ├─ no  → present it / pursue other routes
       └─ yes → are you within 30 days of the dishonour memo?
            ├─ yes → send statutory demand notice now → wait 15 days → if unpaid, complaint within 30 days
            └─ no  → §138 window likely lost → pursue civil/Order 37; verify
```

## Common mistakes
- Sending the demand notice late (after the 30-day window).
- Filing the complaint outside the post-notice window.
- Wrong/incomplete notice content (must demand the cheque amount and reference the dishonour).
- No proof of notice dispatch/service.
- Assuming §138 recovers the money by itself — it pressures payment; plan the civil side too.

## Punishment (for leverage context)
Imprisonment up to **2 years** and/or fine up to **twice the cheque amount**.
