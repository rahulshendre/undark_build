# RBI customer-protection rules — recovery-relevant

## Rule: A working grievance-redress mechanism is mandatory
- **Why it exists:** Borrowers must have a real route to challenge wrong charges or misconduct.
- **Operational implication:** The lender must have a grievance officer and timelines; unresolved complaints can escalate to the RBI Ombudsman. A live dispute can pause or reshape recovery.
- **Evidence needed:** Published grievance contacts; complaint records and responses.
- **Penalty if violated:** Ombudsman intervention; regulatory action.

## Rule: Charges and dues must be accurate and disclosed
- **Why it exists:** Borrowers should not be pursued for opaque or excessive amounts.
- **Operational implication:** The amount demanded must be reconcilable to the agreement, statements, and disclosed charges. Penal charges/interest must follow disclosed terms and applicable rules.
- **Evidence needed:** Loan agreement, statement of account, charge schedule.
- **Penalty if violated:** Disputed amount; weakened recovery; regulatory exposure. TODO: verify current rules on penal charges.

## Rule: No misleading or coercive communication
- **Why it exists:** Protects borrowers from deception and pressure.
- **Operational implication:** Notices and messages must be accurate (correct amount, correct legal basis) and free of false threats (e.g. threatening criminal action where none lies).
- **Evidence needed:** Copies of all notices/communications sent.
- **Penalty if violated:** Conduct violation; a false legal threat can backfire in proceedings.

## Rule: Fair treatment of vulnerable borrowers
- **Why it exists:** Hardship and sensitive circumstances warrant care.
- **Operational implication:** Allow for genuine hardship, death, or dispute; settlement and restructuring exist for a reason. Avoid contact during bereavement or other sensitive events.
- **Evidence needed:** Case notes reflecting circumstances considered.
- **Penalty if violated:** Conduct and reputational exposure.

## Operational takeaway for Undark
Recovery must be built on a clean, reconcilable amount and accurate legal basis.
If the documents do not let you reconcile the outstanding figure, that is both a
risk and a compliance flag — never recommend a notice asserting an unverified
amount.
