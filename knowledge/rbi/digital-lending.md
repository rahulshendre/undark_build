# RBI Digital Lending Guidelines — recovery-relevant points

Scope: loans originated through digital channels / apps, often via a Lending
Service Provider (LSP). Relevant because many small-ticket loans are
digitally originated, and the documentation trail differs.

## Rule: Lending must run through a Regulated Entity (RE)
- **Why it exists:** To keep accountability with an RBI-regulated lender even when an app/LSP fronts the loan.
- **Operational implication:** The actual lender of record is a bank/NBFC; the app/LSP is an agent. Identify the RE — that is who the recovery is for and who is accountable.
- **Evidence needed:** Loan agreement showing the RE as lender; LSP/RE arrangement.
- **Penalty if violated:** Regulatory action against the RE.

## Rule: Key Fact Statement (KFS) and all-inclusive cost disclosure
- **Why it exists:** Borrowers must see the true cost (APR) and key terms upfront.
- **Operational implication:** A KFS with the all-inclusive APR and charges should exist. Its absence is a documentation gap and a potential dispute point in recovery.
- **Evidence needed:** KFS / sanction letter with APR and charges.
- **Penalty if violated:** Disclosure violation; weakens the lender's position.

## Rule: Money flows directly between borrower and RE
- **Why it exists:** To stop LSPs from pooling borrower funds and to keep flows transparent.
- **Operational implication:** Disbursement and repayment should move directly between the borrower's and the RE's bank accounts, not through an LSP pass-through/pool account. Repayment trails should reflect this.
- **Evidence needed:** Bank statements showing direct RE↔borrower flows.
- **Penalty if violated:** Regulatory action; flow irregularities can complicate proof of debt.

## Rule: Cooling-off / look-up period
- **Why it exists:** Lets a borrower exit early without penalty within a set window.
- **Operational implication:** Early-exit terms may affect what is actually owed; check whether the loan was within any look-up period. TODO: verify current cooling-off requirements.
- **Evidence needed:** Loan terms; disbursal and any early-repayment records.
- **Penalty if violated:** Dispute over amount owed.

## Rule: Data and conduct protections carry into recovery
- **Why it exists:** Digital borrowers' data and dignity must be protected.
- **Operational implication:** No misuse of phone contacts/gallery, no harassment via the app or messaging, same conduct rules as fair-practices.md. Contact-list scraping for collection is prohibited.
- **Evidence needed:** Consent records; communication logs.
- **Penalty if violated:** Serious regulatory and legal exposure.

## Operational takeaway for Undark
For app-originated loans: confirm the RE (real lender), look for the KFS, and
verify repayment flowed directly to the RE. Missing KFS or pass-through flows
are recovery risks worth flagging.
