# RBI Fair Practices Code — recovery-relevant rules

Scope: how a regulated lender (and anyone acting for it) may pursue collection.
Only the recovery-relevant provisions are distilled here.

## Rule: Recovery must not rely on harassment or coercion
- **Why it exists:** To protect borrowers from intimidation, force, and public humiliation during collection.
- **Operational implication:** No threats, no use of muscle power, no public shaming, no abusive language. Persuasion and lawful process only.
- **Evidence needed:** Call recordings/logs, field-visit reports, and notice copies that show lawful, non-coercive conduct.
- **Penalty if violated:** Regulatory action against the lender, liability for the lender for its agents' conduct, and possible civil/criminal exposure. TODO: verify current penalty framework.

## Rule: Contact only at reasonable hours
- **Why it exists:** To prevent collection at hours that harass or intrude.
- **Operational implication:** Restrict borrower contact to permitted hours. TODO: verify exact permitted window (commonly cited as not before 08:00 and not after 19:00) against the current source.
- **Evidence needed:** Time-stamped call/visit logs.
- **Penalty if violated:** Regulatory action; complaint grounds for the borrower.

## Rule: Do not disclose the debt to third parties
- **Why it exists:** Privacy; a debt is between lender and borrower.
- **Operational implication:** Do not discuss the debt with relatives, neighbours, employers, or references except as narrowly permitted (e.g. obtaining location information without disclosing the debt). No public notices naming the borrower as a defaulter beyond what law permits.
- **Evidence needed:** Records showing contact stayed with the borrower/guarantor.
- **Penalty if violated:** Privacy and conduct violations; regulatory and reputational consequences.

## Rule: The lender remains responsible for its agents
- **Why it exists:** Outsourcing collection does not outsource accountability.
- **Operational implication:** Any third-party recovery agent's conduct binds the lender; the lender must ensure agents follow the same rules. See recovery-agents.md.
- **Evidence needed:** Agent engagement letters, training records, conduct audits.
- **Penalty if violated:** Regulatory action against the lender for the agent's breach.

## Rule: Transparent terms and a grievance mechanism
- **Why it exists:** Borrowers must understand obligations and have a route to complain.
- **Operational implication:** Loan terms, charges, and the recovery/grievance process should be transparent; a grievance redressal officer/route must exist and be reachable.
- **Evidence needed:** Loan documents, KFS/sanction letter, published grievance officer details.
- **Penalty if violated:** Regulatory action; escalation to the RBI Ombudsman.

## Operational takeaway for Undark
When recommending a next action, the analysis must stay inside these rails:
no coercion, lawful hours, borrower-only contact, documented good-faith effort.
Flag any case where prior conduct (per the documents) may have breached them.
