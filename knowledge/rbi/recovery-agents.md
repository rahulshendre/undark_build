# RBI rules on recovery agents

Scope: when a lender uses a third-party agent/agency to collect.

## Rule: Agents must identify themselves and carry authority
- **Why it exists:** Borrowers must know who is contacting them and that they are authorized.
- **Operational implication:** The agent discloses identity and the lender they represent; carries an authorization letter; ideally provides a copy on request.
- **Evidence needed:** Authorization letter, agent ID, engagement records.
- **Penalty if violated:** Lender liable for unauthorized/misrepresenting agents; regulatory action.

## Rule: Same conduct standards as the lender
- **Why it exists:** Outsourcing must not lower the bar.
- **Operational implication:** Agents follow all Fair Practices Code conduct rules — lawful hours, no harassment, no third-party disclosure, no coercion. See fair-practices.md.
- **Evidence needed:** Call recordings, visit logs, training records.
- **Penalty if violated:** Action against the lender for the agent's breach.

## Rule: Calls may be recorded; borrower may be informed
- **Why it exists:** Accountability and dispute resolution.
- **Operational implication:** Maintain recorded/logged interactions; borrower can be told calls are recorded.
- **Evidence needed:** Retained recordings/logs with timestamps.
- **Penalty if violated:** Weak evidentiary position and conduct exposure if disputes arise.

## Rule: Restrictions on hours, frequency, and place of contact
- **Why it exists:** To prevent harassment through excessive or untimely contact.
- **Operational implication:** Contact only within permitted hours and at reasonable frequency; respect requests and sensitive circumstances. TODO: verify exact permitted hours and any frequency caps against current source.
- **Evidence needed:** Time-stamped contact logs.
- **Penalty if violated:** Complaint grounds; regulatory action.

## Rule: Grievance redressal must be available
- **Why it exists:** Borrowers need recourse against agent misconduct.
- **Operational implication:** Provide and honour a complaint channel; escalation to the lender's grievance officer and the RBI Ombudsman.
- **Evidence needed:** Published grievance contacts; complaint-handling records.
- **Penalty if violated:** Regulatory action; ombudsman intervention.
