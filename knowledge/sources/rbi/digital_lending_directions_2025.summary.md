# RBI (Digital Lending) Directions, 2025 (RBI/2025-26/36)

**Document purpose:** Consolidates the regulatory requirements for digital
lending by regulated entities — borrower protection, fair practices, data
privacy, and loss-sharing.

## Applies to
All Commercial Banks; Primary (Urban)/State/Central Co-operative Banks; all
NBFCs (including Housing Finance Companies); all-India Financial Institutions.

## Key operational takeaways
- For app/online-originated loans, the **RE remains the lender of record**; the
  app/LSP is an agent and the RE guides it (including when it acts as recovery agent).
- The borrower must be **told the particulars of the recovery agent** before contact.
- A **Key Fact Statement (KFS)** with APR is required; grievance redressal is
  structured with a 30-day escalation to RBI.
- A **cooling-off period of not less than one day** lets the borrower exit.

## Important rules (as stated on the page, with clause numbers)
- **Para 5.v — Recovery guidance:** "RE shall impart necessary guidance to LSP
  acting as a recovery agent, to discharge their duties responsibly."
- **Para 8.v — Agent disclosure:** the borrower is given the "particulars of
  such recovery agent authorised to approach the borrower."
- **Para 8.iii — Document flow:** digitally signed documents "shall
  automatically flow to the borrower on the registered and verified email/SMS
  upon execution."
- **Para 8.i — KFS:** "RE shall provide a Key Fact Statement (KFS)" (per
  DOR.STR.REC.13/13.03.00/2024-25).
- **Para 11.i–iv — Grievance:** RE and LSP designate nodal grievance redressal
  officers, displayed prominently; complaints unresolved within 30 days can be
  escalated to RBI's CMS portal.
- **Para 10.i — Cooling-off:** borrower may exit by paying principal +
  proportionate APR without penalty during an initial cooling-off period "not
  being less than one day."

## Sections relevant to loan recovery
- Para 5.v and 8.v (recovery-agent conduct and disclosure for digitally
  originated loans).
- Para 8.i (KFS — the document whose absence is a recovery risk).
- Para 11 (grievance — a live complaint can reshape recovery).
- Para 10.i (cooling-off — affects what is actually owed).
